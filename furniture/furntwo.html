Concrete Lego Table

Scroll to get building..

 

 

A LEGO Project for Adults!

Materials:
- LEGO Bricks
- Quikrete Countertop Mix - Hot Glue Gun

 Step 1: Build the Mold
 Build your mold brick by brick and when you get to the top be sure to add som support braces. This will keep the walls from bending out under the weight of the Quikrete. For the Lego base plate, use several small plates rather than one large one, it will make it easier in the long run.

 Step 2: Mix and Pour the Quikrete
 Mix the concrete and begin to fill your mold. Make sure that after adding about three inches of concrete, use a stick to push the mix into the corners of your mold (do this every three inches or so). Also shake in periodically to release any air bubble in the concrete.

 Step 3: Smooth and Level
 Once your mold is full, use a wide putty knife to smooth out the surface. This step does not matter too much because the Lego pattern will be in the front.

 Step 4: Let it Cure
 Let it cure.

 Step 5: Remove the Mold
 This process can become a bit time consuming. The easiest way that we have found to remove the pieces is by flipping it over and using needle nose plyers to remove the Legos. Be careful though, you don’t want to damage those precious little bricks of endless fun.

 Step 6: Cleaning the Bricks
 Cleaning the bricks is fairly easy. Just but them in a mesh bag and throw in in the washing machine. Be sure to only use cold water though as warm water will warp the bricks. If you don’t have access to a washing machine cold water, non-abrasive soap and a toothbrush work just fine.
